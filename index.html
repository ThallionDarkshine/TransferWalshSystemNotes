Balanced NT Openings

  • With a weak hand, and either a long suit (5+ major or 6+ minor) or length in both majors, transfer/bid stayman and pass.

  • With stronger hands with major length, also start by transferring/bidding stayman, then make an appropriate rebid to show shape and strength.

  • With invitational to game forcing hands looking to play in NT, invite through the 2!S range ask transfer or simply bid 3 NT.

  • With slam invitational hands looking to play in NT, ask about aces with 4!C gerber or invite with 4 NT.

  • With stronger hands not looking to play in NT, start by either showing shortness or transferring to your long minor.

Gazzilli

After 2!C Gazzilli, responder either shows a positive, setting a game force opposite a strong opener, or makes a natural shape-showing negative, looking for a playable strain. Preference for partner's major should be shown in most cases, even with a singleton. A strong opener without a 6 card major will scramble to find a playable contract.

Positive Responses

After a simple positive response, continuations are natural and shape-showing for the most part. Rebidding the original major shows a natural 2M rebid. All other continuations are game forcing. After an invitational positive, game contracts are to play, with all other continuations showing 17+ and slam interest.
